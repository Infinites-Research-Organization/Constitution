This document outlines our foundational governance framework and core research principles.

## Issues Pending Upcoming Vote

* **Mitigation of External Influence:** Establishing strict protocols to prevent government or corporate funding from compromising our research neutrality.
* **Technological Boundaries:** Defining absolute limits on what we will not develop (e.g., mass-surveillance tools, automated cyber-weapons).
* **Publication Architecture:** Deciding between a strict "Open Source by Default" policy versus a case-by-case model incorporating Coordinated Vulnerability Disclosure (CVD).

---

## Research Focus & Goals

Our mission is to build, audit, and open-source technologies that protect individual privacy and enhance digital self-defense.

### 1. Cybersecurity & Privacy Engineering

* **Auditing Utilities:** White-hat penetration testing and network security tools.
* **Privacy Software:** End-to-end encrypted communication and metadata-stripping frameworks.

### 2. Anti-Surveillance Countermeasures

* **Signal Mitigation:** Research into radio-wave blocking, RF-absorbing materials, and physical shielding.
* **Algorithmic Defense:** Development of adversarial machine learning patterns (AI-Camouflage) to disrupt unauthorized facial recognition systems.

## Platform & Legal Compliance

*   **Dual-Use Content:** All tools and research are created strictly for educational, defensive, and privacy-preserving purposes.
*   **GitHub Policy Alignment:** In compliance with GitHub's Active Malware or Exploits Policy, this organization explicitly supports dual-use security research and does not use the platform as attack infrastructure or for malicious malware distribution.
